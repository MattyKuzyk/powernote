Endive and avocado salad with fennel vinaigrette

Serves 8; adapted from Smitten Kitchen

  • 2 teaspoons fennel seeds, toasted
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 3 tablespoons finely chopped shallot (1 large)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 pound frisée (French curly endive), torn into bite-size pieces (10 cups)
  • 3 Belgian endives, cut crosswise into 1/2-inch-wide slices
  • 2 avocados, thinly sliced

Grind fennel seeds in spice grinder. You don't want it to be too fine. Transfer to a small bowl or cup, then stir in oil until combined. Let stand 15 minutes.

Whisk together lemon juice, shallot, salt, and sugar in another small bowl until salt and sugar are dissolved. Stir fennel oil. Add the shallot mixture slowly, whisking as you go along.

Toss lettuces and avocado in a large bowl with vinaigrette. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Shrimp cakes with roasted garlic-orange aioli

Makes 8 patties; adapted from FOOD52

Roasted garlic–orange aioli

  • 2 heads garlic
  • 1 cup plus 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 4 large egg yolks, room temperature
  • 1 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Kosher salt
  • 4 tablespoons freshly squeezed orange juice plus 2 teaspoon finely grated zest (from 1 orange)
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Preheat the oven to 400 degrees. Peel away the outer skin of 2 garlic heads, and cut a little off the top to expose the individual cloves. Drizzle 2 tablespoons of olive oil over the garlic heads, wrap in foil, and roast until cloves are soft when pressed. This should take about 30 minutes. After the cloves have cooled, squeeze them out of their skins. Mash 6 cloves and set aside.

To make the mayo, whisk the egg yolks in a medium bowl. Gradually whisk in the remaining olive oil until the mixture becomes very thick. Add a pinch of salt and the orange juice and zest. Whisk in the roasted garlic paste slowly. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Shrimp cakes

  • 10 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped fennel, from one small bulb (set aside the fronds)
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped shallots
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped red bell pepper
  • 4 tablespoons finely chopped fennel fronds
  • 2 tablespoon finely chopped chives
  • 2 tablespoon zest from 1 orange
  • 3 pounds shrimp, peeled and deveined, finely chopped by hand
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2-4 tablespoons roasted garlic-orange aioli

Heat 4 tablespoons olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the fennel and shallots and cook until they start to caramelize, about 8 minutes. Add the red bell pepper and cook for another 3 minutes, then remove from heat. Add in the fennel fronds, chives and orange zest.

Combine this mixture with the shrimp in a medium bowl. Fold in the aioli a little at a time. Add salt and pepper to taste.

When ready to cook, form into patties and pan-fry over medium heat with olive oil. Depending on the size of the patty, it should take about 8 minutes to cook through. Make sure you cook them evenly on both sides.

Strawberry Rhubarb Crumble

8-12 servings; adapted from 101 Cookbooks

  • Butter for greasing skillet/pan (about 1 tablespoon)
  • 3/4 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon fine grain sea salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 lb hulled medium strawberries, cut into quarters
  • 12 ounces trimmed rhubarb, sliced into 3/4-inch pieces

Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F.

Combine the flour, oats, sugar, salt, and pepper together in a bowl. Stir in the butter and form the mixture into small chunks. Spread these out on a sheet of wax paper and chill for 20 minutes in the freezer.

Mix the strawberries and rhubarb together. Add the cornstarch and sugar and toss until the fruits are evenly coated. Add this filling to a buttered baking dish.

Crumble the chilled chunks over the filling. Try to have a mix of sizes, but make sure the entire top is covered.

Bake until the topping is deeply golden. This should take 35-40 minutes. Let cool slightly, but you'll want to serve this warm.
