Introduction to Quip

Overview

Quip is a collaborative word processor and messaging system. It's available on iPhones, iPads, and your desktop. You can access it at  ﻿https://quip.com/﻿.

What is Quip?

Quip combines an easy-to-use docu ment editor with messaging tha @ t's similar to text messaging. You can start a conversation with people, create a document, and edit collaboratively — each participant sees the same text and can make edits in real time. Or, you can create a private document, work on it for awhile, and then share it with others once you're done.

This conversation and document combination is called a thread. You can add as many people as you want to the thread.

Editing documents

Editing documents with Quip is easy. Just start typing, and use the style menu to make changes to your style or layout. To find the style menu, look for this symbol on your desktop: 


If you are editing on your phone or tablet, the style menu is attached to the top of the on-screen keyboard:


Use the style menu to choose whether the line of text you're working on is a Paragraph, Heading, or List. Headings can be small, medium or large, and Lists can be bulleted, numbered or checklists. 


When writing or editing a list, you can use Tab to indent a list item, or Shift+Tab to de-indent a list item. To exit List mode, use the style menu to switch back to plain Paragraph.

Keyboard shortcut: Tap the backtick key (to the left of the 1⃣ key) to cycle through the style options until you hit the one you want.

Mentioning people and docs

Quip makes it easy to insert links to other things by using mentions. Mentions, or @mentions, are inserted any time you type the @ symbol.


For instance,  Peter  is a person mention.  Introduction to Quip  is a mention of this document.

Try it: To see how it works, type after the symbol: @

Sending messages

To send a message on the current thread, click on the text box to the left of the document. 


Type your message in the box, and hit Enter to send, similar to text messaging or instant messaging. You can add a line break to your message using Shift-Enter if you want to type a longer message.

On the iPhone, you'll need to drag your document to the see the message thread.


Try it: Click in the message box, type “Hello,” and hit send. You'll see the message show up next to this document.

Sharing your document

Once you've written a document, it's easy to share it with others. Just click on the Sharing icon above the document conversation.


You can share with your Quip contacts by typing their name. On your phone and tablet, you can also share with people in your address book. They'll get an email or text message with a link to the document.

Diffs

Every time an edit is made to a document, a diff, or a document edit, is added to the thread. The diff visually shows you exactly what's been changed. When someone else edits a document, diffs make it easy to stay up-to-date without re-reading the whole document.


In a diff, green text indicates new words that have been added, and red indicates words that have been removed.

Inbox

The Inbox shows you the documents that others have shared with you. You can think of it as “what's new.” It will show you new messages, new docs, and edits you haven't seen yet.


Just like in email, there's an indicator when something is “unread”. Things you haven't seen yet are marked with a blue dot.

Desktop

The Desktop is where your documents and folders live. You can think of it as “your stuff.”


When using Quip on the web, you can drag documents around to organize them.

To get to your Desktop on the iPhone, drag down anywhere on the Inbox.


Creating a document

To create a new document, click on the blue "plus" in the bottom right corner of your Desktop:


Creating folders

You can also organize your documents into folders. A folder can be either private (i.e. just for you), or shared. If you share a folder with your family or your team at work, everyone will have access to the same set of documents.

Creating a folder

To create a folder, press the folder button at the top right corner of the Desktop.


Sharing a folder

When you share a folder, other people can add documents or contribute to the documents already in the folder. To share a folder (or add new participants), open the folder in the Desktop, and tap the Sharing icon.


On the iPhone, tap the gear icon and select “Share Folder.”

Archive

Once you have more than a few documents, your Desktop can easily get cluttered. To clean up your desktop, you can archive the documents you aren't currently using.


On the iPhone or iPad, you can archive a document by long pressing it in the Desktop.

You can access your previously archived documents from the Archive folder, or by searching for them by name.
